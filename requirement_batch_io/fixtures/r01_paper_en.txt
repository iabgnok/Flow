Title: On Tiny Language Models for Workflow Integration Testing

Abstract. This note serves as a stand-in for an English academic paper body. We argue that deterministic UTF-8 fixtures accelerate regression testing when the execution stack only exposes text-oriented skills such as file_reader and llm_translate-style steps. Binary PDF ingestion would require separate adapters; here the “paper” is plain text so pipelines can be exercised without OCR.

Keywords: workflow, integration testing, localization, fixtures

Section 1. Introduction
Continuous validation benefits from corpora that are small yet structurally realistic: titles, abstracts, sections, and keywords mirror real submissions while remaining diff-friendly in Git.

Section 2. Method
We compose synthetic sections with stable token boundaries so that translation or summarization steps have predictable anchors for golden checks in CI.

Section 3. Limitations
Results do not generalize to scanned documents or equations embedded as bitmaps.

Section 4. Conclusion
We recommend validating pipelines with small synthetic corpora before scaling to full proceedings-sized PDFs.
